Emily Dion - My Coding Journey

About Me

Hey, there! My name is Emily, and I am from Edwardsville, IL. Originally from the small town of Newton, IL, I moved here to attend SIUE in 2021, which I graduated from in 2023 with a bachelors in Mass Communications and a minor in Computer Science. I am a driven, caring, and fun-loving individual, who loves travelling, playing cards, doing puzzles, and geeking over movies. I am also an avid Denver Broncos fan. My goal is to become a full stack developer, a true blend of my logical and creative talents.

So, why development?

First Exposure

In fifth grade, I had a fantastic teacher named Ms. Hundley. Not only was she a heavily influential in my life, but she also gave me my first introduction to programming through doing lessons on code.org. While it wasn't an immediate click, I definitely knew that it resonated with my technical, puzzle-solving, skillset. The interest was sparked.

Getting Started

Unfortunately, I was a part of the unlucky high school graduating class of 2020... a year of dread for just about everyone. The pandemic made for an interesting first year of college, with my first semester being entirely online. For someone who was undecided in their major at the time, it forced me to sort through SIUE's entire course catalogue to find anything that worked. In my search, I found CS101: Introduction to Programming. I loved it, and decided to declare Computer Science as my major.

Changing Paths

In my CS track, one of the courses was Database & Web System Development. In this class, we learned the very basics of web development, like HTML, CSS, and basic SQL. I was hooked! However, looking forward at the rest of my major-related coursework, it didn't seem like much else applied to that specific track. However, the Mass Communications department (which I had a minor in at the time), had two web design classes available. Thinking that this would put me on a better path, I switched majors entirely. In the meantime, I scouted out relevant work experience. Through this, I landed a position doing freelance work for a small remote company called CloudCarpenter. My boss, Shane, developed his own CMS for his clients to use. My role was "Front End Developer", where I helped to build out templates for the users to start their site with, and also organized foundational pages for clients who didn't want to do everything themselves. This was really great experience for someone just starting out, and I am super grateful for it.

Now, We're Here...

Thinking I had done everything to be on the right path toward a job in web development, I was shocked when all of the jobs required a Computer Science degree (or equivalent training). While I shouldn't have been entirely surprised by this, I was disheartened when I found that no companies seeking web developers would even look at my application. However, this led me to look at more design-focused positions, which I am currently working in now. While design is fun, and I excel in that role, I miss development. With a career in development, I hope to be challenged every day, and feel fulfilled by solving complex problems in creative ways. Now, LaunchCode will allow me to get the position that I have been dreaming of for some time now.
